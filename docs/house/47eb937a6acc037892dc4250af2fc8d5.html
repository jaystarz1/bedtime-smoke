A Little for You, a Little for Me

In Hush Hollow's garden, all tucked in and green, where the softest of breezes drift gently between, two friends, Pup and Fox, in the hush of the day, came out to the garden to wander and play.

Beside the old fence, in the warm evening light, a berry bush waited, all rosy and bright. "Oh, look," whispered Pup, "at this wonderful find!" And the berries hung ready, all sweet and all kind.

But soon down the path came the others to see, so Fox opened a hand: "There is plenty for tea. A little for you, a little for me."

Then they gathered the berries, so gentle, so slow, and passed them around in a soft, sleepy row. Enough for the robins, the bees, and the wren, enough to share round, and then share it again.

Old Dandelion came, with a nod and a smile, and rested nearby for a warm little while. "How lovely to share," murmured Dandelion low. "The sweetest of treasures are sweeter to show." A berry for this one, a berry for those — a little for you, a little for me.

Now each little friend had a berry to keep, and the garden grew quiet and ready for sleep. The bush had enough, and the sharing was done, and the joy was the biggest when shared by each one.

So Pup and Fox wandered back home through the green, past the drowsy old garden, all peaceful, serene. The Moon settled soft in the hush of the sky, and the stars kept their watch as the night drifted by.

A little for you, a little for me — now hush, little garden, and sleep, sleepy tree.

Goodnight to you, Pup. And goodnight to you, Fox. Goodnight, old Dandelion. Goodnight, garden rocks. Goodnight to the Moon, up above and around. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Sleep safe and sleep sound.
