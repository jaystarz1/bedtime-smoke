Just You and Me, Soft and Slow

In Hush Hollow, the evening comes down soft as a blanket. Over the grass. Over the little roofs. Over every small one going home.

Hedgehog stood at the door with one wish, and it was not a big one. To sit by the pond with Pup, and let the evening happen.

So Hedgehog went along the path and knocked on Pup's door, gentle, gentle.

"Will you come to the pond?" said Hedgehog. "We won't do anything grand. Just you and me, soft and slow."

Pup came out and took Hedgehog's hand. "Just you and me," said Pup. "Soft and slow."

Down the path they went, past the sleepy hedges, past the last warm window. The grass was cool. The pond lay still and shining, holding the whole sky on its back.

They sat down side by side.

Mr. Trout came up under the water and made one quiet ring. He turned his speckled side to the last of the light, and his tail swayed slow, slow, slow.

"Good evening, you two," said Mr. Trout. "And what great thing are you doing tonight?"

"Nothing great," said Pup. "Only this."

"Ah," said Mr. Trout, and he sank down into the dark, kind water. "That is the very best thing there is."

So they stayed. They dipped their fingers in and brought up beads of dew from the reeds. They hummed a tune with no words in it, the kind that hums itself. The stars came out over the pond, one, then two, then too many to count.

And Hedgehog leaned in and said, quiet as the water, "Will it always be like this?"

Pup held on tight. "Wherever you go," said Pup, "I will come and sit beside you. However far, I will always find you. Just you and me, soft and slow."

The night was warm around them. Hedgehog's shoulders went easy. Two friends by the water, doing nothing at all, and full right up with it.

Then home they went, hand in hand, up the path to Hush Hollow, where the lamps were low and the beds were waiting.

"Goodnight, Mr. Trout," they said. "Goodnight, dew. Goodnight, reeds. Goodnight, Moon."

"Goodnight, Pup," said Hedgehog.

"Goodnight, Hedgehog," said Pup.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Close your eyes now. Just you and me, soft and slow.
