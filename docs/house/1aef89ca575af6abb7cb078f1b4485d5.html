Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In Hush Hollow, where the willow leans low, the Pond lay still and dark and warm. Mouse and Bunny sat at the water's edge, side by side, with the whole big day still humming inside them.

"I cannot settle," said Mouse. "My day is too full."

"Mine too," said Bunny. "It will not lie down."

The day spun and bounced and would not be quiet.

Then Frog came paddling, slow and round and green, and folded his long legs on a lily pad. "When my day will not lie down," said Frog, "I count the soft things, one by one. Watch."

Frog blinked his wide gold eyes up at the sky.

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

Mouse tipped her face to the sky. Bunny tipped her face too.

Above the Pond, the stars came out one at a time. Mouse breathed in slow. Bunny breathed out slow.

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

Three stars now, over the willow. Four stars, on the water. Frog counted them gentle and low, and with each one the day grew smaller and softer and farther away.

"Breathe in the cool," said Frog. "Breathe out the day."

Mouse breathed in the cool Pond air. Bunny breathed out the long, full day. The humming hushed. The bouncing slowed.

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

Five stars. Six. The Moon rose over Hush Hollow, quiet and pale, keeping her own slow watch. The Pond held her light like a cupped hand.

Mouse's eyes grew heavy. Bunny leaned warm against her. The day had drifted off across the water, and Mouse and Bunny drifted with it, soft and slow, soft and slow.

Frog tucked his legs beneath him. "Goodnight, Mouse," he said. "Goodnight, Bunny."

"Goodnight, Frog," they whispered.

Goodnight, stars. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, quiet Pond.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
