Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In Hush Hollow, where the grass grows soft and the path winds slow, there is a garden. Bunny came to the garden as the sky went dim.

By the garden wall, someone small was hiding. Just two eyes in the shadow of a leaf. The garden was cool and green, with the scent of fresh leaves. A cricket sang one slow song, then went quiet, too.

"Hello," said Bunny, soft and low. "I am Bunny. Will you come and sit with me?"

The small one stayed in the shadow.

So Bunny sat down in the cool grass and waited. Bunny did not rush. Little by little, little by little, the small one came a step closer.

Dandelion grew tall at the edge of the path, her golden head nodding in the quiet breeze. "Take your time," whispered Dandelion. "No one hurries here."

"What is your name?" asked Bunny.

"Hedgehog," said the small voice, softer than a whisper.

"Hello, Hedgehog," said Bunny. "The dark is kind here. The dark is just the day, resting. I will sit right here, as long as you like."

Little by little, little by little, Hedgehog came out from the leaf and sat beside Bunny in the grass.

Together they sat, soft and slow. The breeze went still. The garden held them gently. Dandelion let her seeds drift up like little stars. The garden was warm. The garden was safe.

"Are you my friend now?" asked Hedgehog.

"I am," said Bunny. "I will be here tomorrow, too."

And little by little, little by little, the night grew soft around them, and a yawn came, and another.

Bunny leaned close. Hedgehog leaned close. Dandelion folded her golden head to rest.

Up above, the Moon held her quiet light over Hush Hollow, soft over the garden, over the grass, over two new friends.

Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Dandelion. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
