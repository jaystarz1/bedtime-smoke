Slower Now, Softer Now

Down in Hush Hollow, at the edge of the wide green Meadow, the day had been a busy, buzzing day. Fox and Mouse had run and hopped and played from morning until the sky turned gold. Now their legs were tired and their eyes were heavy, but the busy day buzzed on and on inside them, and it did not want to be put down.

Robin fluttered down from the hawthorn, her feathers fluffed soft against the evening. "A busy day needs hushing," she trilled, "one small thing at a time. Slower now, softer now."

So Fox and Mouse sat side by side in the tall sweet grass, and they hushed the busy things, one by one.

First the bees stopped their buzzing and tucked into their hive.

"And the bees are asleep," said Fox, "all snug in the Meadow."

Then the brook slowed its chatter to a whisper, then a hum, then a hush.

"And the bees are asleep, and the brook is asleep, all snug in the Meadow," said Mouse.

Then the wind lay down in the grass and let the tall stems be still.

"And the bees are asleep, and the brook is asleep, and the wind is asleep, all snug in the Meadow," said Fox and Mouse together.

Slower now, softer now.

Robin folded her wings and nestled into her nest of moss and twigs. Her song grew small and round and sleepy, and then it was only breath.

"And the bees are asleep, and the brook is asleep, and the wind is asleep, and Robin is asleep, all snug in the Meadow."

The Moon came up over the Hollow and kept a gentle light on everything below.

Fox yawned a big slow yawn. Mouse yawned one too, because yawns are for sharing.

Slower now, softer now.

Hand in hand they went home through the dozy grass, back to warm blankets and pillows plumped just right. The busy day was quiet now. Calm settled over the Hollow like a blanket, tucked in at every corner.

"And the bees are asleep, and the brook is asleep, and the wind is asleep, and Robin is asleep, and Fox is nearly asleep, and Mouse is nearly asleep, all snug in the Meadow, all snug in the Hollow."

Slower now, softer now.

"Goodnight, Fox," said Mouse.

"Goodnight, Mouse," said Fox.

"Goodnight, Robin," they said together, soft as the grass.

"Goodnight, Moon."

"Goodnight, Hush Hollow."

And everything slept.
