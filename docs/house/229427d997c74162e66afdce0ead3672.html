Goodnight, Hush Hollow

At the end of the day in Hush Hollow, when the light goes low and the grass turns cool and grey-green, Bunny and Hedgehog go up the Hill.

They go slowly. There is nothing at the top to hurry for. There is only the wide sky, and the first star, and a good place to sit down.

But tonight Hedgehog's thoughts are going fast. They hop and skip and will not sit. "My thinking won't lie down," says Hedgehog. "It keeps getting up again."

"Then we'll give it a little job," says Bunny. "A slow one. Slow jobs are the best kind."

So they make a soft place to sit, there at the top of the Hill, the way you make any good thing — a little at a time. A handful of dry grass. Then another handful. Bunny gathers. Hedgehog smooths it flat. It is too big a job for one, and just right for two.

And while they work, they breathe.

*In and out, soft and slow.*

A handful of grass, in and out. Another handful, soft and slow. Nobody counts. Nobody hurries. The Hill is patient, and the two of them are patient with it.

Above them, Wink comes out. Wink is the first star, small and dreamy, keeping watch over the Hill. Wink brightens. Wink dims soft. "I'll keep the light on," says Wink, faint and far and warm, and gives a slow, slow wink that means *take your time.*

So they take their time.

*In and out, soft and slow.*

One more handful of grass, and one more, and then the soft place is done. Bunny sits. Hedgehog sits. They lean together, tired and glad, the way you are when a thing is finished and finished well.

"Where did it go?" says Hedgehog. "My fast thinking?"

"It floated off," says Bunny. "That happens when you breathe it out."

And it has. Hedgehog's thoughts are lying down now, one by one, quiet as the grass. Eyes go heavy. Wink dims low. All of Hush Hollow is settling into the dark, and the dark is a kind one.

*In and out, soft and slow.*

So goodnight now, from the top of the Hill.

Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Wink, keeping the light on. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, grass, and the soft place, and the little job well done.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
