Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In Hush Hollow, when the sky turned soft and blue, Mouse and Bunny went walking down the Wood path, hand in hand.

Bear came along the path the other way. He had made a basket that morning — reeds and long grass, woven round and round, with a handle that curved just so. It was heavy with blackberries.

Then the handle gave way. A small sound, a quiet one. The basket tipped. The blackberries rolled into the moss, one, and one, and one.

Bear sat down on a stump. His great shoulders sank. His rumble went quiet.

Mouse sat down beside him. Bunny sat down on his other side.

"We can mend it," said Mouse.

"Gentle, gentle," said Bunny. "All is well."

So they gathered the blackberries, one, and one, and one, back into the basket. Bunny carried the basket. Mouse carried the handle.

Down by the stream they pulled three long reeds and dipped them in the cool water until the reeds went soft and bendy.

Then Mouse held the handle steady, and Bunny wove a reed over, and under, and over. Slow hands, and slower still. The way you do a thing when you want it to hold.

"Gentle, gentle," said Mouse. "All is well."

Over. And under. And over. The reeds went tight. The handle went strong.

Bunny tied the last knot with careful hands, and set the handle back on the basket, snug where it belonged.

Bear lifted it. He carried it home, swinging soft, and his rumble came back — low and warm and glad, like a kettle on a stove.

Something warm stayed behind them in the Wood. A small, quiet warm, like a lamp left lit in a window.

Back in the Hollow, the fireflies drifted low. Bear set his mended basket down by his door. Mouse yawned. Bunny yawned too, a long slow yawn, and the yawn went round like a passed-along blanket.

"Gentle, gentle," whispered Bunny. "All is well."

Over the Wood, the Moon kept her quiet watch, and the stars kept theirs.

Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Bear, and goodnight, basket, mended and whole by the door.

Goodnight, Moon.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
