Up the Little Hill and Home Again

In Hush Hollow, when the sky went soft and dim, the lamps in every window glowed warm and low. Mouse and Bunny sat by the door, snug and sleepy, as the last of the light settled over the Hill.

"I wonder what's up there tonight," said Mouse, "just a little way up the Hill."

"Not far," said Bunny. "Only a little way, and then home."

So they took a small warm bun from the table, wrapped it up cozy, and out they went, up the gentle Hill. Out and home, soft and slow.

The grass was cool and soft beneath them. The Hollow glowed small and golden below. And up on the quiet Hill, a tiny light blinked on and off, on and off. It was Wink, the firefly, glowing all alone in the dark.

"Hello, Wink," said Mouse softly. "We brought you something warm."

Bunny set the bun down in the grass beside him. A little kindness, soft and small. Wink glowed a little brighter, and the whole Hill glowed warmer for it.

They stayed a while, the three of them, as the stars came out one by one. No hurry. No far to go. Just the soft grass, the cool night air, and the glow blinking slow.

Then Mouse gave a yawn, and Bunny gave a yawn, and Wink blinked slow and sleepy.

"Time to go home," said Bunny.

"Home," said Mouse.

And down the gentle Hill they went, back toward the warm windows of the Hollow. Out and home, soft and slow.

Wink floated along beside them, glowing low, all the way to the door.

Inside, the beds were soft and waiting. Mouse settled in warm. Bunny settled in warmer. And the whole night felt a little softer for the kind thing they had done. Out and home, soft and slow.

Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, little Wink, glowing low. Goodnight, Moon, up over the quiet Hill.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
