Goodnight, Hush Hollow

The whole day in the Wood had been a busy, buzzing day.

Hedgehog had run and tumbled. Pup had splashed and played. Round and round the Hollow they went, all day long, until the sky went soft and the light went low.

Now the day did not want to be put down. It hummed in their paws. It buzzed in their ears.

Mrs. Squirrel came down her tree, slow and sure, her tail curled like a question. "Come," she said. "It is time to make a busy day into a quiet night."

Slower now, softer now.

First, the wind. It had pushed and played all day. Mrs. Squirrel hushed it with one paw, and the leaves went still. Hedgehog goes still too, and leans into the warm.

Slower now, softer now.

Next, the little stream. It had hurried and rushed all day. Mrs. Squirrel sang it a low, round song, and the water went slow and quiet. Pup comes and sits, and rests one tired paw.

Slower now, softer now.

Then the birds, who had chattered all day, tucked their heads beneath their wings. The crickets dimmed their tiny tune. One by one, the busy things were hushed, and the Hollow grew soft as a folded blanket.

Hedgehog yawned a small, round yawn. Pup yawned a long, slow yawn. The buzzing in their paws was gone. The hum in their ears was gone.

The Moon came up over the Wood, gentle and kind, holding her light for all the sleepy ones below.

Mrs. Squirrel pulled the moss up close. "There," she said. "The fast day has become a soft, slow night. Calm settles over everything, like a blanket over the Wood."

Slower now, softer now.

Goodnight, Hedgehog, curled in a cozy ball. Goodnight, Pup, warm and still. Goodnight, Mrs. Squirrel, in your tall, quiet tree. Goodnight, Moon, watching over the Wood.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
