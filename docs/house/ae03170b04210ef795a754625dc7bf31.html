Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

By the soft side of the Stream, where the water went slow and slow, Bunny came down to the bank. Hedgehog came too, small and sleepy, leaning close.

Hush Hollow was warm. The reeds swayed. The Moon was up, quiet over the water.

Bunny found something then. A berry — round and ripe and shining red, the best berry in all the Hollow. Bunny held it up. It glowed like a little lamp.

But Hedgehog wanted a taste. And up popped a beetle, who wanted a taste. And along the bank came a mouse, who wanted a taste too.

"There is one berry," said Bunny, soft and slow. "And there are so many of us."

From the cool of the Stream rose Mr. Trout, his fins fanning gentle in the dark water. His big eyes were kind. "Then share it out," he said, in his deep, slow, bubbly voice. "A berry split is a berry doubled."

So Bunny split the berry. A little for Hedgehog. A little for the beetle. A little for the mouse. A little for Bunny.

A little for you, a little for me.

Each one took a piece, no bigger than a dewdrop, and oh — it was sweet. Hedgehog smiled. The beetle hummed. The mouse twitched her whiskers. Bunny laughed a small soft laugh.

"See?" said Mr. Trout, sinking slow into the cool. "No one goes without."

A little for you, a little for me.

The Stream went slow and slow. The reeds swayed. Bunny leaned against Hedgehog, and Hedgehog leaned against Bunny, and the last of the sweetness sat warm on every tongue.

The Moon laid a soft ribbon of light across the water. Everything was still. Everything was shared.

A little for you, a little for me.

Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Mr. Trout, down in the cool of the Stream.

Goodnight, Moon, over the quiet water.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
