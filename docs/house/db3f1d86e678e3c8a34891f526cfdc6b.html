The Meadow at Night

In Hush Hollow the windows go golden, then gray, and the meadow lies folded at the end of the day. Pup stands at the doorway where the grass starts to grow, where the dark spreads out softly. Pup does not want to go.

For the dark is so wide, and the dark is so deep, and it holds all the places the daytime would keep. Then Fox comes up softly, and Fox reaches a hand. "We will take one small step. Then we'll stop. Then we'll stand."

And Robin calls down from her nest in the pine, where her small ones are folded in one feathered line: "The meadow at night is the meadow by day. Soft and safe, soft and safe," sings the Robin. "This way."

So one step. Then another. The grass is cool and tall, and nothing at all happens. Nothing at all. Then a lantern goes drifting, so slow and so small — a firefly. Then two. Then a hundred in all.

And the crickets are tuning their little green strings, and the wind through the meadow says quiet, kind things. And the Moon is above them, and quietly there, and the dark is not empty. The dark is just air.

They stand very still where the meadow is deep. The meadow leans close and says nothing but sleep. No corners. No edges. No hurry. No sound, just fireflies lifting, and cool, quiet ground.

Then Pup breathes out slowly and stands in the glow, with Fox one step near, and the fireflies low. "It is only the daytime with blankets," says Pup. And the fireflies answer, and lift themselves up.

Then Fox and Pup wander back home through the dew, and Robin flies with them, the whole small way through. And the door of the Hollow is warm and is wide, and the dark comes politely and waits just outside.

Soft and safe, soft and safe, say the boards of the floor. Soft and safe, says the blanket. Soft and safe, says the door.

Goodnight to the Robin, asleep in her pine. Goodnight to the fireflies, folding their shine. Goodnight, little Fox. Goodnight, little Pup. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. The stars are tucked up. Soft and safe, soft and safe. Now the long day is done. The meadow is sleeping, and so is the sun.

This is the house voice. On a voice tier, the very same story is read in your voice, with your child's name woven in.
