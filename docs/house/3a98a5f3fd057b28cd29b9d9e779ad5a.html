Just You and Me

In Hush Hollow, where the meadow holds the last warm light of the day, Mouse went to find Bunny.

"Will you stay with me tonight?" Mouse said. "Not to run, not to play. Just to sit in the soft grass, you and me."

Bunny came close and leaned in beside Mouse.

"Just you and me, soft and slow," said Bunny.

So they sat, side by side, in the tall green meadow. The grass swayed slow around them. The day went quiet and cool.

Down from the hedgerow hopped Robin, her round feathers fluffed for evening. She tucked her wings and gave one soft, sleepy whistle, a little goodnight song for the Hollow.

"Look," said Mouse. The first dewdrop sat on a blade of grass, round and silver and still. Then another. Then more, all across the meadow, each one holding a tiny shine.

Bunny held very still. Mouse held very still. Robin smoothed one feather and settled.

"Just you and me, soft and slow," they hummed together.

Above the meadow, the stars came out, one by one, easy and slow. And the Moon rose over Hush Hollow, watching the quiet grass below.

They did nothing grand. They did not need to. They had the dew and the stars and the warm grass, and they had each other, and that was the whole of it.

When the meadow grew cool, Mouse gave a little yawn. Bunny gave a little yawn too. Robin had already tucked her head beneath her wing.

So they walked home together, soft and slow, back through the grass to the warm and cozy Hollow, where the beds were soft and the night was kind.

"Just you and me, soft and slow," Mouse whispered, and Bunny was already half asleep.

The meadow breathed. The stars held still. The Moon kept her gentle watch.

Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Robin.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
