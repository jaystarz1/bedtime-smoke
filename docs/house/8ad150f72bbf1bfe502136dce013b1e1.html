Goodnight, little one

In Hush Hollow, the evening came soft and slow. The reeds leaned low. The pond lay still and dark and cool. Fox came down the quiet path. Mouse came along beside. And there, on a wide flat stone at the water's edge, sat Frog, round and green and calm, blinking his big gold eyes.

"Tonight," said Frog, "is a first-time night."

At the edge of the pond floated one small lily pad. It was a new thing to step upon, and a little daunting, the way new things are. Fox looked at the lily pad. Mouse looked at the lily pad. The lily pad bobbed, soft and slow.

"You do not have to hurry," said Frog. "One small step, soft and slow."

So Fox went first. Fox leaned close to the cool water. Fox held very still, and reached one paw to the pad. One small step, soft and slow. The lily pad dipped, then held. Fox stood upon it, calm and proud, with the whole pond glowing quiet all around.

Then Mouse came near. Mouse held tight to a reed. Mouse breathed in the green and watery air, and reached out too. One small step, soft and slow. The lily pad rocked, gentle as a cradle, and held. Mouse stood beside Fox, both of them brave, both of them still.

"There," said Frog, low and warm. "A new thing, done. Now there is nothing left to do but rest."

So Fox sat. So Mouse sat. So Frog folded his green legs on the wide flat stone. The reeds whispered, soft and slow. The water held the sky. And up above the sleeping pond, the Moon came out to keep the watch, quiet and kind over Hush Hollow.

A yawn went round the little pond. The frogs grew hushed. The reeds grew still. One small step, soft and slow, and the long brave day was done.

Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Frog on your flat warm stone. Goodnight, pond. Goodnight, Moon.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
