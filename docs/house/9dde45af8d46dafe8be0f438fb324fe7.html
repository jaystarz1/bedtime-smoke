Near and Dear

The stream in Hush Hollow went soft and slow, soft and slow, over the smooth grey stones.

Mouse sat on the bank with a cup of hollow-water and a stone in each hand. Bunny came along the path and sat down too, one step away, then two.

"You live up the hill," said Bunny. "I live down by the reeds. That is a long way apart."

Mouse thought about the hill, and the reeds, and all the soft grass in between.

"That is a long way," Mouse agreed.

So they sat, one step apart, and the stream went soft and slow, soft and slow.

Frog came hopping up the bank with dew on his green back and settled on a warm flat stone. He tucked his toes beneath him and blinked his round gold eyes.

"What are you two doing so far apart?" said Frog.

"We are from different ends of the Hollow," said Bunny.

"Mmm," said Frog. "And what do you hear, up your hill?"

Mouse listened. "The stream," said Mouse. "At night, when everything is quiet. The stream goes soft and slow."

Bunny sat up. "Down in the reeds, I hear it too. Every single night."

Frog blinked. "Well then," said Frog. "It is the same water. It goes past your reeds and under your hill and past me, all night long. You have been listening to the same song this whole time."

Bunny moved one step nearer. Mouse moved one step nearer too.

Near and dear, near and dear.

They put their two flat stones side by side in the shallow part, where the water made a small bright ring around each one. Two stones, touching. The stream went over them, soft and slow.

"Yours is the grey one," said Mouse.

"Yours is the greyer one," said Bunny, and they both went quiet and warm the way you do when a thing is settled.

The sky above Hush Hollow turned the colour of a folded blanket. The moon came up over the trees, quiet and kind, watching the water go by.

Bunny walked Mouse to the top of the path. Mouse walked Bunny back down to the reeds. Then they said goodnight in the middle, which was exactly halfway, and it was not far at all.

Near and dear, near and dear.

In the reeds, Bunny lay down and listened. Up the hill, Mouse lay down and listened. And the stream went soft and slow, soft and slow, the same song in two warm places at once.

Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Frog on your warm flat stone.

Goodnight, moon. Goodnight, stones, side by side in the shallow water.

Near and dear, near and dear.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
