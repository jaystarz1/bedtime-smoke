Goodnight, Bunny and Hedgehog

Down by the Pond in Hush Hollow, the day grew slow and soft. Bunny and Hedgehog had picked a small basket of berries, one by one, together and slow. Now the basket was full, and their eyes were tired, and the sky went dim.

"Time to go home," said Bunny.

The path home was long for two small ones and one full basket. So they carried it between them, a hand on each side.

Together, together, soft and slow.

Old Frog came hopping up the bank, his webbed feet slapping the cool mud, his throat a low and friendly rumble. "I will walk you home," croaked Frog. "A little more, and a little more."

So they went. Bunny held one side. Hedgehog held the other. Frog hopped beside them, glug, glug, glug.

Together, together, soft and slow.

The reeds bowed low. The water lay still and quiet. A cricket sang its one small song. Up above, the Moon came out, quiet and kind, keeping her gentle watch over the Pond.

"Not far now," said Hedgehog.

"Not far now," said Bunny.

And there, past the last of the reeds, a little light glowed warm in the window. Home. Someone had left the lamp on. Someone was waiting.

Together, together, soft and slow.

They set the basket down by the door. They leaned in the doorway, tired and glad. Warm arms came around them, and a warm voice said, "There you are. I kept the light on for you."

That is the best thing of all, to come home after a long and gentle day, to someone who loves you.

Frog gave one last happy rumble. "Goodnight," he croaked, and hopped off soft into the dark, toward his own cozy bit of Pond.

Bunny leaned back. Hedgehog leaned back. The berries were home. The light was warm. The day was done.

A little more, and a little more, and then it was done.

Goodnight, Frog. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Moon, keeping watch over the still, dark Pond. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.

Hush now. Rest now. Soft and slow.
