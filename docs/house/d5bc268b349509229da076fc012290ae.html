The Little Cove Step

In Hush Hollow, where the path winds down to the water, there is a quiet Cove. The sand is soft. The waves come slow. And there, at the edge of the evening, sat Bunny and Hedgehog, side by side.

Tonight there was a small new thing to do. Out in the shallow water stood a flat, smooth stone, just far enough to feel far. Bunny wanted to stand on it. Hedgehog wanted to, too. But the water was new, and new can feel big.

Mr. Trout swam up in a curl of silver, his fins waving slow. "One small step," he said. "Soft and slow."

So Bunny reached out a hand and set one foot in the cool, gentle water.

One small step, soft and slow.

Then Hedgehog came near and set one foot in, too. Together, together, soft and slow. The water was not scary at all. It was warm at the top and cool below, and it held them kindly.

A little more, and a little more. One soft step, then another. Mr. Trout stayed beside them the whole way, humming a low water-hum.

One small step, soft and slow.

And then, there they were. Both of them, standing on the flat, smooth stone, tired and glad. They had done the new thing. They had done it slow and brave.

"Well done," said Mr. Trout, and he dipped below in a happy little ripple.

Bunny and Hedgehog waded back, hand in hand, up the soft sand to the warm dry blanket at the top of the Cove. They settled down, snug and side by side, proud of the small brave thing.

One small step, soft and slow.

The Cove grew hushed. The waves went quiet. High above the water, {{moon}}, keeping soft watch over the still, sleepy Wood.

Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Mr. Trout, down in your cool green deep.

Goodnight, Cove. Goodnight, Moon.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
