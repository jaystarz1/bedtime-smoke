For You, For You

In Hush Hollow, where the meadow grass grows soft and slow, the evening came down warm and gold. Hedgehog and Pup sat close together in the long grass, near the last light of the day.

Between them lay one ripe berry. Just one. The best one, round and sweet, saved from all the rest.

"For you," said Hedgehog, holding it out. "For you, for you."

Pup smiled. But high on a fencepost, Robin was singing a small tired song, and the song had gone thin and slow. Robin had flown far that day, over the hills and home again, and now Robin had nothing left to eat.

Hedgehog and Pup turned to each other. They did not mind. Not one bit.

"For Robin," said Pup softly. "For you, for you."

Hedgehog carried the berry up the grass and set it down gently by Robin's little feet. Robin dipped his head, and his feathers puffed, and he ate the sweet berry to the very last. Then Robin sang again — a warm, round song, the happiest song in all the Hollow.

And here is the thing, my love: giving that berry away felt better than keeping it ever could. A warm glow spread through Hedgehog and through Pup, warmer than the berry, warmer than the gold evening. That is the quiet secret of a shared thing. It comes back to you as light.

Robin sang them a sleepy song. The meadow grass swayed, soft and slow, soft and slow.

Hedgehog yawned. Pup leaned close. And above them the Moon came up over Hush Hollow, quiet and kind, keeping her gentle watch.

Wherever you go, my loves, and however far, you carry this warm glow inside you. It is yours to keep, and yours to give — for you, for you.

Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Pup. Goodnight, Robin, up on your fencepost. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.

This is the house voice. On a voice tier, the very same story is read in your voice, with your child's name woven in.
