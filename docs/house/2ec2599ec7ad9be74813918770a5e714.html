The Lost Little Shell

In Hush Hollow, where the path bends down to the Cove, the night came soft and slow. The water lay quiet. The sand lay cool. The Moon set a long silver road across the waves.

Honey sat at the edge of the tide, her paws still, her whiskers low. Honey had lost her little shell — the pink one, the smooth one, the one she carried to bed.

Fox came down the path. Mouse came after. They sat beside Honey in the hush.

"We will find it," said Fox. "We will look," said Mouse.

So they began. Look low, look slow.

By the rocks they went, soft and slow. Fox bent to the dark stones. Mouse leaned by the little pools. A crab slept under a leaf of green. A gull dozed on a high grey ledge. Goodnight, crab. Goodnight, gull. But the little shell was not there.

Look low, look slow.

By the dune grass they went, soft and slow. Fox parted the long blades. Mouse stepped between the reeds. A beetle curled in a curl of bark. A snail rested on a cool round stone. Goodnight, beetle. Goodnight, snail. But the little shell was not there.

Honey's paws grew tired. Honey's eyes grew small. Still Fox held her warm, and Mouse stayed near.

"One more place," said Fox. "By the water," said Mouse.

Look low, look slow.

Down by the gentle tide they went. And there, in the wet and glimmering sand, the Moon laid down her light — and the little pink shell shone back.

Honey gathered it close. Honey held it tight. "Thank you," she whispered, soft as foam. "Thank you, thank you."

Back up the path they went, soft and slow, to the warm of the Hollow, to the moss and the wool. Honey tucked the shell beneath her chin. The waves hushed on below.

Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Honey, and the little pink shell. Goodnight, Moon, on the long silver sea. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Sleep now. Sleep slow.
