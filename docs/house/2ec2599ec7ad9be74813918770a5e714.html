Goodnight from the Cove

In Hush Hollow, where the water lies still and the reeds stand soft, there is a quiet Cove. The pebbles are cool. The little waves come slow. Hush, says the water. Hush, hush.

Here is the Cove, all tucked for the night. Here are the pebbles, grey and round. Here are the reeds, tall and still. Here are the little crabs, folded up and dreaming. Hush, hush.

Tonight Honey sat by the edge with her paws in her lap, and her bright eyes went soft and worried. "I have lost my little shell," said Honey. "The smooth one, the round one, the one I love best."

Fox came close. Mouse came close. "We will help you," they said. "Look low, look slow."

So they began.

Mouse went along the wet stones, one by one, slow and careful. Fox leaned over the cool sand. Honey padded behind them, soft on her round bear feet, hoping and hushing.

Look low, look slow.

They searched by the smooth grey stones. They searched where the little crabs sleep. They searched under the soft green reeds, and beside the cool damp leaves, and along the edge where the water hums.

Look low, look slow.

Under a reed — only a beetle, asleep. Beside a log — only a curl of moss. The water came in, slow and kind, and went out again. Nothing to fear here. Only the search, soft and patient, by the light of the stars.

Mouse held back a leaf. Fox brushed a little pile of sand. Honey leaned in close, her warm fur glowing in the quiet.

Look low, look slow.

And there — in a small dip near the water, where a pebble had rolled — something round, something smooth, catching the silver light.

"My shell," whispered Honey. She gathered it up in her paws and held it to her heart. "You found it. You stayed and you searched. Thank you, thank you."

Fox sat down. Mouse sat down. Honey settled between them, warm and round and happy, the little shell safe again.

The Cove grew still. The reeds nodded. The waves came slow, slower, slow.

Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Honey, with your shell held close. Goodnight, Moon, up over the quiet water. Goodnight, little stars keeping watch.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Sleep now. Hush, hush, hush.
