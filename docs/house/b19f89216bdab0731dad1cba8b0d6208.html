Goodnight, Hush Hollow

Here is the Wood, soft and dim. Here is the path home. The evening is warm. The evening is still.

Here is Fox, by the old stone. Here is Mouse, by the round root. Today a little thing went wrong. A basket of berries, meant to share, tipped and spilled in the leaves. Each thought the other had turned away. So they sat apart. So the quiet grew.

There is a small space between them. A quiet, empty space. It is not so wide. It is only wide tonight.

Bear comes along the path, slow and low, with a rumble like a far-off drum. Bear's warm shadow falls over them both. "Friends," says Bear, soft as fur, "a spill is only a spill."

Fox takes one step. Mouse takes one step. And there they are, side by side again.

"I am sorry," says Fox, gentle and small. "I am sorry too," says Mouse. The words are soft. The words are enough.

They gather the berries, one by one, into the mended basket. One for Fox. One for Mouse. One for Bear, who rumbles a thank-you. And the little space between them is gone, and they are side by side again.

The Wood grows dark and kind. {{moon}} Home is close now. Home is warm.

Fox goes home along the quiet path. Mouse goes home along the quiet path. Bear settles low beneath the trees. And all of Hush Hollow is soft, and slow, and ready for sleep.

So here is the goodnight round.

Goodnight, Fox, warm in your bed. Goodnight, Mouse, warm in yours. Goodnight, Bear, with your gentle rumble. Goodnight, berries in the mended basket. Goodnight, quiet path. Goodnight, kind and sleepy Wood.

The friends are side by side again. The evening is warm. The evening is still.

Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.

This is the house voice. On a voice tier, the very same story is read in your voice, with your child's name woven in.
