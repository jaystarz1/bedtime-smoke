Tonight's Hush Hollow story

Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

Here to listen for 24 hours.

Goodnight, Little Stream

Here is the Hollow at night. Here is the grass, gone soft. Here is the path down to the water.

Here is Fox. Here is Mouse. They carry a blanket between them, one corner each.

The blanket is wide. The blanket is warm. Warm and near, warm and near.

Here is the stream. It goes slow. It says hush over the stones. Hush, and hush, and hush.

Here is a flat grey stone at the water's edge. Here is Frog, sitting on it, damp and small, his throat going in and out. Frog has no blanket at all.

"The night is cool," says Frog. "Cooler than I like."

Fox lifts one corner. Mouse lifts the other.

"Then come under," says Mouse.

Here is Frog, hopping in between. Here are three at the edge of the water, side by side, under one wide blanket.

Warm and near, warm and near.

The blanket does not grow smaller. Three are under it now, and it is warmer than it was with two. Warmth is like that. Share a little, and there is more.

Here is the water, going by. Here are the reeds, leaning. Here is a firefly, blinking once, then twice, then done.

Frog sings one low note. It rolls out over the stream and comes back quiet.

Now their eyes are heavy. Now it is time for beds.

Mouse has a small square of wool in a pocket, soft and grey. Mouse tucks it around Frog, snug from chin to toes.

"This one stays with you," says Mouse.

So Frog is warm on his stone. So Fox and Mouse fold the big blanket and go up the path, slow, and slower, and home.

Here is the Hollow again. Here are the small bright windows. Here are the beds, turned down and waiting.

Warm and near, warm and near.

Now the round of goodnight.

Goodnight, stream, going slow over your stones. Goodnight, reeds. Goodnight, firefly. Goodnight, Frog, snug in your grey wool. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, stars. Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.

The Hollow is warm. The Hollow is still.

Sleep now. Sleep now. Warm and near.

This is the house voice. On a voice tier, the very same story is read in your voice, with your child's name woven in.
