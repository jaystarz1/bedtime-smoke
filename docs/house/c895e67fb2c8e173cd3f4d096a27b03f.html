The Meadow

In Hush Hollow, where the grass grows soft and the Moon keeps watch, Bunny and Hedgehog stayed up past the sleepy hour.

A small voice came from the hawthorn. "Oh dear," said Robin. "Oh dear, oh dear." She had a nest to line before the cold came down, and a heap of thistledown to carry, far too much for two small wings.

"We can help," said Bunny. "We can help," said Hedgehog.

And so they began, together, together, soft and slow.

Bunny goes to the thistledown and gathers a little. Hedgehog comes behind and holds a little more. Up the gentle path they carry it, one soft bundle, then another, while Robin hops beside them with a thank you in her throat.

Together, together, soft and slow.

The Moon climbed higher. The grass cooled. Bunny sits to rest, and Hedgehog leans close, and Robin tucks each puff of down into the curve of her nest.

"Almost," said Robin softly. "Almost, almost."

So they carried the last of it, together, together, soft and slow.

When the nest was full and round and warm, Robin settled in. "I could not have done it alone," she said. "And you could not have carried it alone. But two small ones can do what one cannot."

Bunny smiled. Hedgehog smiled. The job was done, and they were tired in the good, proud way.

Back through the Meadow they went, home to the Hollow, where the burrow was warm and the moss was deep. The Moon hung quiet above the hawthorn, a gentle light over every small and sleepy thing.

Bunny settles into the soft. Hedgehog leans into the warm. Outside, the thistledown nest held Robin close, and Robin's eyes grew heavy too.

Hush now. The work is finished. The Hollow is still.

Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Robin in your round warm nest. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
