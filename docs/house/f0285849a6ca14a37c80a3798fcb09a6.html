Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In Hush Hollow, when the sky went soft and gray, two friends went up the Hill. Hedgehog and Pup climbed slow, hand in hand, to say goodnight to the day.

But the Hill was high, and home was down below. The windows of the Hollow grew small and far.

"I miss home," said Pup, in a little voice.

Beside them, a warm glow woke in the grass. It was Wink, blinking gold, soft and slow.

"I'm here," said Wink. "Sit near me. I'll show you something."

They sat. Wink glowed, warm as a candle.

"When you love a place," said Wink, "you carry it inside. Home isn't only down the Hill. It's tucked in you, wherever you go. Always near, always dear."

Pup put a hand on their heart. "In here?"

"In there," said Wink. "Always near, always dear."

The dark came kind and quiet. Up above, the Moon rose, and the stars kept their gentle watch. The Hill was not scary now. It was cozy, like a blanket laid over the world.

"See?" said Wink softly. "The dark is a friend. It only wants you rested."

Hedgehog leaned near, warm and sleepy. "I have home right here."

"That's the love," said Wink. "It stays. Always near, always dear."

They sat a while, the three of them, the glow between them steady and small. A little breeze went by, slow and sweet. Somewhere below, the Hollow waited, warm.

"Time to go down," said Wink. "Home is waiting, and home is with you. Both at once."

So they walked down the Hill, soft and slow, Wink glowing just ahead to light the way. Down to the Hollow, down to the warm, down to the beds that knew them.

Hedgehog yawned. Pup yawned too. They tucked in side by side, snug and safe, with the love they carried folded around them like a quilt.

"Goodnight, Hedgehog," said Wink. "Goodnight, Pup."

"Goodnight, Wink," they whispered.

Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, stars keeping watch. Goodnight, the love that stays.

Always near, always dear.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
