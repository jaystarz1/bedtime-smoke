The Littlest One in the Garden

In Hush Hollow, when the sky went soft and grey, the garden began to settle. Hedgehog and Pup came down the little path, hand in hand, past the drowsy flowers and the slow, cool grass.

At the end of the path waited Dandelion, the littlest one, with down as soft as seed-fluff and a yawn as wide as the whole evening. Dandelion was much too sleepy to get ready for bed alone.

"We'll help you," said Hedgehog. "We'll help you," said Pup. And so they did, soft and sure, the way you'd want someone to help you.

Pup poured a little cup of warm milk and held it steady. Dandelion drank it slow, one sip, then two, then a tiny hum of thank-you.

Hedgehog dipped a soft cloth in cool water and wiped the day from Dandelion's cheeks, gentle as could be. "There," said Hedgehog. "All clean, all cozy."

Hush now, snug now, the garden holds you.

Hedgehog smoothed a bed of clover, round and deep, and patted it flat. Dandelion climbed in and curled up small, nose tucked under one downy wing.

Pup brought a leaf-blanket, green and cool, and pulled it up to Dandelion's chin. Hedgehog found the softest bit of moss to be a pillow.

Hush now, snug now, the garden holds you.

Above them, the Moon came out to keep the watch, quiet and kind. "Wherever you sleep," whispered Hedgehog, "someone is holding the night for you." And Dandelion's eyes went slow, and slower, and closed.

The garden breathed. The crickets sang their small, low song. Hedgehog and Pup leaned close together and yawned, one and then the other.

Now they were sleepy too. So they lay down beside their little friend, in the clover, under the leaf and the moss and the wide soft dark.

Hush now, snug now, the garden holds you.

Goodnight, Dandelion, dozing warm. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Pup. Goodnight, Moon, keeping watch. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Sleep now, sleep deep. The night will keep you till morning.
