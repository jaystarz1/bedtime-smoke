Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In the Garden of Hush Hollow, the day folded away, soft and slow. Mouse came down the warm path, and Bunny came too, close beside. Side by side, the way good friends go.

By the old Dandelion they stopped to rest. Bunny sat down small. The light slipped low between the leaves, and the Garden grew wide and dim. A little shake went through Bunny, head to toe.

"I don't like the dark," Bunny said. "It is so big out here."

Mouse came near. Mouse sat close beside, and leaned in soft and slow.

"You are safe," Mouse said. "You are safe."

Then Mouse made a little job for two. "We will tuck the Garden in," Mouse said. "Side by side, soft and slow."

So they did. Bunny held a leaf, and Mouse smoothed it down. They folded the clover low. They patted the moss to bed. They hushed the little stones in a row. Soft and slow, soft and slow, the way good work is done.

The old Dandelion gathered her seeds in close, a round white blanket, pale and glowing. She nodded over the two small friends and kept her quiet light.

Up above, the Moon came out, soft over the sleepy Garden.

"See?" Mouse said. "The Garden holds us. The Dandelion glows. The Moon is up there too."

Bunny leaned on Mouse, warm and near. The big dark was not so big now. The shake was gone. Bunny gave a long, slow yawn.

"You are safe," Mouse said again, soft and slow. "You are safe."

And Bunny was. Side by side they stayed, in the warm and quiet Garden, while the Dandelion glowed and the Moon kept watch.

Slowly, slowly, their eyes grew heavy. Slowly, slowly, the Garden went still.

Goodnight, Mouse. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Dandelion, glowing soft and low. Goodnight, Moon.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
