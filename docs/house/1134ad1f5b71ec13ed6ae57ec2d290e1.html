You Are Safe

On the soft green Hill above Hush Hollow, where the grass goes silver in the evening, Hedgehog sat down beside Pup.

The day had folded itself up small. The Hollow below was going quiet, one warm window at a time. Somewhere down there, a kettle sang, and then even the kettle went still.

Pup was looking at the dark coming in over the far trees.

"It gets so big," said Pup, in a small voice. "The night gets so big."

Hedgehog did not say hush, and did not say silly. Hedgehog just sat nearer, side by side, so that their shoulders touched, and stayed there.

"You are safe," said Hedgehog. "You are safe."

And Pup let out one long breath.

Hedgehog had a blanket, the soft grey one with the loose thread that Pup liked to hold. Hedgehog opened it and put it around them both, one half of it over Pup, one half over Hedgehog, and tucked the edge in tight.

"Now the night is only as big as this blanket," said Hedgehog.

Above them, Mr. Crow settled on the crooked fence post, black and glossy, and folded his wings the way an old friend folds a newspaper.

"Evening, small ones," said Mr. Crow.

"Pup says the night is big," said Hedgehog.

Mr. Crow tipped his head and thought about it the slow way he thinks about everything.

"It is," he said. "Big and old and full of sleeping things. Every one of them tucked in somewhere. Every one of them held."

He ruffled once and grew still.

Pup leaned into Hedgehog's shoulder. The blanket was warm now, warm all the way through, warm the way bread is warm.

"You are safe," said Hedgehog again. "You are safe."

And this time Pup smiled and said it back, quiet as a moth: "You are safe."

Below them the Hollow breathed. The grass leaned. The fence post creaked once and then decided not to.

Pup's eyes were heavy. Hedgehog's eyes were heavy too.

"However far you go," said Hedgehog, "I will sit beside you. That is the whole of it. That is all I know how to do."

"That is enough," said Pup.

And it was.

The two of them sat under one blanket on the soft green Hill, side by side, going slower and slower, until the sitting turned into leaning and the leaning turned into sleep.

Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Pup.

Goodnight, Mr. Crow, on your crooked fence post.

Goodnight, moon.

You are safe. You are safe.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
