Goodnight, Hush Hollow

In Hush Hollow, near the quiet Cove, the water lay still and dark. Honey settled the two of them on the warm sand and tucked a soft blanket snug around them.

Pup gave a long, slow yawn and leaned in tight. But Hedgehog sat up, wide awake, still full of the whole big day.

"I can't go soft yet," said Hedgehog. "My day is still buzzing and buzzing."

"Then we will let it go, slow and easy," said Honey, with a low warm hum. "I'm right here, soft and safe. Look up with me."

Above the Cove, the sky was deep and kind. The Moon rested low, growing slim and sleepy, and the little stars held the light.

"First, let's name the calm things," Honey said softly. "The still water. The warm sand. The slow, slow waves. Your own slow breath."

Hedgehog breathed in, and breathed out.

"Now we count the stars, one at a time," Honey hummed. "Ready?"

Hedgehog nodded and held Honey's warm paw.

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

"There," said Honey. "That one is for the games you played today. Let it drift off now."

Hedgehog breathed out. One buzzing thing floated up and away, over the still, dark water.

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

"That one is for the paths you walked," Honey hummed. "Set it down. It will keep till morning."

Pup was already dreaming, warm and heavy against the blanket. Hedgehog leaned near, eyes growing soft and slow.

"I'm still a little awake," Hedgehog whispered.

"I'm right here, soft and safe," said Honey. "We have all the stars we need. One more."

"One star, two stars, soft and slow."

And with that one, the last of the day drifted off, up past the sleepy Moon, and Hedgehog drifted with it.

Honey gathered the two of them and carried them home through the hush, back to the warm den in the Hollow. Blankets up. Breathing slow. The Cove behind them, quiet and safe.

"Goodnight, Pup," Honey hummed. "Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, little stars. Goodnight, Moon."

"Goodnight, Hush Hollow."
