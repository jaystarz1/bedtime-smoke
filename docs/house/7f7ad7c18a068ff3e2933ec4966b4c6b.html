Slow and Soon

By the Stream in the Hollow, where the tall grasses lean, Pup and Fox sat down softly by the water, cool and green. Mr. Trout swam up slowly with a flick and a shine. "There's a gift on its way, you two. It will come in good time."

What was it? A glimmer, a something to see, drifting down with the current, slow as slow as can be. And the Stream gave a whisper in its old water tune, "It is coming, it is coming, slow and soon, slow and soon."

So they waited. They rested. They were still as a stone. Pup counted the ripples. Fox hummed a low tone. A dragonfly settled. A reed dipped its head. The willow bent over and made a soft bed. No need to hurry, no need to run, for good things arrive slow and soon, slow and soon.

Then around the wide bend, where the water runs deep, came a small leaf of gold with a glow it could keep. A firefly rode it, aglow and at ease, the loveliest lantern to drift on the breeze. It had taken its time, and the wait made it sweet, and the calm made the coming a gift and a treat.

Mr. Trout gave a nod. "Now off home you should go." So they walked to the Hollow, soft and slow, soft and slow. The Moon rose up gently to light the Stream's gleam, and the whole quiet Hollow grew soft as a dream.

Goodnight, Mr. Trout, in your deep silver pool. Goodnight, little firefly, your lantern so cool. Goodnight to the willow, the reeds, and the Stream. Goodnight, Pup and Fox, drifting off into dream. Goodnight to the Moon, drifting soft through the sky, and slow and soon, slow and soon... close your eyes. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Goodnight.
