Goodnight, Pup and Fox

The lanterns of Hush Hollow flickered down low, and the Pond lay as still as a mirror of snow. Then Pup came with a bucket, and Fox with a light, to say to the water a gentle goodnight.

Mr. Trout at the shallows blew one silver sigh. "My pebble-step tumbled when rain hurried by. I have fins, I have ripples, but hands I have none, and this work is too heavy to do all alone."

So Pup lifted one, and then Fox lifted two, and they set them down where the small ripples grew. No hurrying, no rushing, just row upon row. Together, together, soft and slow.

They laid the flat grey ones in front of the reeds. They tucked the small round ones in snug with the weeds. A cricket kept time with a small sleepy tune, and the reeds bowed their heads to the wind's little croon.

One stone was too heavy. It would not go alone, so four hands went under and lifted the stone. They counted it softly, and let the stone go. Together, together, soft and slow.

Mr. Trout swam his step, and his fins gave a shine. "A stair for the shallows! And every stone fine! I never could build it with fins, don't you see, but two pairs of hands made it easy for three."

Then home up the path where the lantern-moss glows went two sleepy helpers on two sleepy toes, with arms warm and tired and hearts all aglow. Together, together, soft and slow.

Goodnight to the Pond and the reeds where they grew. Goodnight, Mr. Trout, in your water of blue. Goodnight to the Moon and her quiet white light. Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Pup. Sleep warm and sleep tight. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. The whole Wood is still, and the stars keep their watch over meadow and hill.

So close your eyes now, as soft as the snow, and drift away, drift away, soft and slow.
