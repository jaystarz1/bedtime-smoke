A Step on the Hill

Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

On the Hill in Hush Hollow, where soft grasses grow, sat Hedgehog and Pup in the evening's warm glow. And Wink the small firefly blinked gold in the air, a flicker, a shimmer, a wink here and there.

But earlier, somehow, a small thing went wrong. A word came too quickly. A quiet stayed long. So Hedgehog sat this way, and Pup sat from there, with a soft little sadness afloat in the air.

They both wished to mend it. They both wished to say the warm little words that had wandered away. But the Hill stretched so wide, and the words were so few, and neither one knew quite the right thing to do.

Then Wink floated over, so gentle, so slow, and blinked a soft question, a small golden glow. "A step," winked the firefly. "One step is the start." So Hedgehog stood up with a brave little heart.

And Pup, from the far side, came soft through the green, till they met in the middle where moonlight would lean. "I'm sorry," said Pup, in a voice low and small. "I'm sorry," said Hedgehog. "I'm sorry for all."

And there on the Hill they were side by side again, the muddle all melted, no need to explain. Wink glowed all around them, a warm little ring, as soft as a hush, as a song you might sing.

Then down went the two through the cool, quiet grass, past sleepy slow petals where night breezes pass. Home to the Hollow, where warm blankets stay, side by side again at the end of the day.

The Moon rose up gently, a glow up above, and lit the whole Hollow with quiet and love. The stars settled in, soft and round overhead, the dark like a friend as they drifted toward bed.

"Goodnight, little Hedgehog." "Goodnight, gentle Pup." "Goodnight, little Wink," as your light softly wraps up. Goodnight to the Hill where the soft grasses bend. Goodnight, Hush Hollow. Side by side again.
