Room for One More

Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

Part of your first week at Hush Hollow.

The light was going low, and Bear had one last job to do. "Winter's coming slow," he rumbled, "and my den wants lining — soft, for the cold nights. But it's a big job for one bear alone." "We'll help," said Fox. "We'll help," said Hedgehog.

So down to the mossy bank they went, the three of them together. Fox gathered soft moss in his arms. Hedgehog carried small bundles, careful and slow. Bear took the big loads, easy and warm.

Together, together, soft and slow.

Up to the den, and down again. Up to the den, and down again. A little moss, and a little more, and a little more than that. Nobody hurried. Nobody fussed. They just worked, side by side, as the dark came down kind. Fox tripped once on a root and laughed; Hedgehog steadied the bundle so not a wisp was lost. "Slow is fine," rumbled Bear. "Slow gets it done."

Together, together, soft and slow.

Bit by bit, the den grew soft and deep and warm — a thick green bed of moss, enough for a long cold winter, with room to spare. "There," said Bear, with a long, glad sigh. "Done. And done well, thanks to you two."

They sat back in the doorway, tired the good kind of tired. The moss smelled green and clean, and the whole warm den was theirs to be proud of. The moss was soft. The work was done. The stars came out, one by one. "Room for one more?" asked Fox, with a yawn. "Always," said Bear. "Always room for one more."

So they stayed, the three of them, snug in the new-lined den.

Together, together, soft and slow.

Now it is time for the goodnights. Goodnight, deep soft bed of moss. Goodnight, the day's work, all done. Goodnight, Bear, in the warm, deep den. Goodnight, Fox. Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
