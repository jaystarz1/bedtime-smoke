Goodnight, little one

Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

Part of your first week at Hush Hollow.

Down in Hush Hollow, where the Cove holds the water still and dark, the night came soft and slow.

Hedgehog came down to the water's edge. Pup came too, and sat near.

"I want to stay near tonight," said Hedgehog. "Near and warm, until the sleepies come."

"Then stay near you shall," said Pup, and leaned in beside him.

In the Cove, Mr. Trout swam up from the deep, his silver scales glowing under the water. He blew one slow bubble, round and bright, and it floated up to the top.

"Hush now," said Mr. Trout, his voice like a ripple. "The Cove is calm. The night will keep you. You are loved and safe, loved and safe."

Hedgehog held Pup's paw. The water lapped, soft and slow. Mr. Trout swam a slow circle, round and round, and the little waves rocked like a cradle.

"Sing the soft song," said Pup.

And Mr. Trout sang, low and gentle, a song with no hurry in it. The reeds swayed. The water hummed along. And the song said, again and again: you are loved and safe, loved and safe.

Hedgehog's eyes grew heavy. Pup leaned nearer still. The Cove was warm. The night was kind.

Up above, the Moon rested over the Hollow, and her light lay soft on the water. The stars kept their quiet watch.

Mr. Trout blew one last bubble, slow and round. It drifted up and up, and popped without a sound.

"Time to rest now," he whispered. "I will keep the Cove. The Moon will keep the sky. And you are loved and safe, loved and safe."

Hedgehog yawned. Pup yawned too. They settled near, warm and still, where the water rocked them soft and slow.

The Cove went quiet. The night held them gently, the way it always does.

Goodnight, Hedgehog. Goodnight, Pup. Goodnight, Mr. Trout in your deep, dark Cove. Goodnight, Moon. Goodnight, stars.

Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
