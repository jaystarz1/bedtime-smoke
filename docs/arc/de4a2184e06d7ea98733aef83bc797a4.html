The Sharing Basket

Read in a Hush Hollow house voice · ages 2–5

In the Meadow, under the early moon, Pup and Bunny found a basket of glowberries — round and soft and warm, each one a tiny light. "Look how many!" said Pup. "Enough to share," said Bunny. "Enough for everyone."

So they carried the basket through the Meadow, and shared it out.

A little for Frog, who plopped up from the reeds — and a little for Frog made the basket no less full.

A little for Frog, and a little for Honey, who came winding through the grass with a sleepy purr — and still the basket glowed.

A little for Frog, and a little for Honey, and a little for Robin, who fluffed down from her branch for her share — and still, somehow, there was plenty.

And a little held up high for Wink, the first star — who could not hold a berry, of course, but blinked, glad just to be asked — and still the basket glowed, as full as when they began.

A little for you, a little for me, a little for everyone.

Pup took one. Bunny took one. And there were berries yet to spare. "How can it still be full?" asked Pup. "Because we shared it," said Bunny. "Sharing made it more, not less."

And there they all sat — Frog, Honey, Robin, Pup, and Bunny — each holding a small warm light, in a ring under the moon. The glowberries shone. The Meadow was soft. Everyone had enough, and everyone was near. Frog gave a small, glad croak. Honey gave a purr. Robin tucked her head beneath her wing.

A little for you, a little for me, a little for everyone.

Now it is time for the goodnights. Goodnight, glowing basket. Goodnight, Frog. Goodnight, Honey. Goodnight, Robin. Goodnight, soft Meadow under the moon. Goodnight, Pup. Goodnight, Bunny. Goodnight, Hush Hollow.
